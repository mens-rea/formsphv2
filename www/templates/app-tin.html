General

Any person, whether natural or juridical, required under the authority of the Internal Revenue Code to make, render or file a return, statement or other documents, shall be supplied with or assigned a Taxpayer Identification Number (TIN) to be indicated in the return, statement or document to be filed with the Bureau of Internal Revenue, for his proper identification for tax purposes (Sec. 236 (i) of the Tax Code).

Procedures

1. Know your classification

Each type of TIN application has different requirements. Once you are aware of the type of TIN you are applying for; whichever is necessary, online or from the BIR.

1901: self-employed and mixed-income individuals.

1902: individuals earning purely compensation income, or non-resident citizens or residents or resident allen employees.

1903: corporations and partnerships.

1904: one-time taxpayer or transact with any government office.

2. Accomplish your application form

After receiving or printing the form that you need, fill it out with valid and accurate information.

3. Submit the requirements

Submit your application form together with the documentary requirements depending on your application. Do not hesitate to ask the office or visit the BIR website (bir.gov.ph) for a list of documentary requirements.

4. Claim your tin

After a short processing time, your name will be called to claim your TIN ID.
