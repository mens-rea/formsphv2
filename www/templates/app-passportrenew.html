General

A Philippine passport is both a travel document and a primary national identity document issued to citizens of the Philippines since there is no national identity card system in the Philippines. It is issued by the Department of Foreign Affairs and Philippine diplomatic missions abroad, with certain exceptions.

Procedures

1. Make an appointment

To set an appointment with the DFA, you can either go to the nearest DFA office or visit the DFA Appointment Website. Whether done online or physically you will need to fill-out or present an already filled-out application form. Once finished, you will be given a reference number-REMEMBER THIS NUMBER.

2. Prepare all required documents

Bring all of the required documents listed in the requirements section. Additional requirements may be needed for NEWLY-MARRIED WOMEN, SPOUSE OF FOREIGN NATIONALS, and MUSLIM applicants.

3. Appear on your scheduled appointment

Applicants must come on the designated time or come early as their slot can be quickly replaced. Bring your filled-up application form and present it to a DFA representative. You will be given a queue number. Wait until your number is called.

4. Go through the process

Once your applicant number is called, you will be directed by a DFA official through a short process of finalizing your application.

5. Process payment fee

Applicant will be asked to pay a processing fee of Php 950.00 at the cashier for regular processing and will take about 15 WORKING days exclusive of holidays. A rush processing is also available at Php 1,200.00 for only 6 WORKING days EXCLUSIVE of holidays. If the application is for a lost and VALID passport, applicants will pay an additional Php 200.00 (please note that the DFA advises a 6 month allowance prior to travel in renewing your passport.)

6. Encoding of Bio-Data

Once all fees are secured, applicants will proceed to the ENCODING SECTION where their bio-data (fingerprints, picture, etc...) will be recorded and saved.

7. Wait for the release of your passport

Applicants must wait for either 7 or 15 days, more or less, depending on the volume of applications. Applicants can recieve their passport physically at the DFA branch or send it to their homes via a delivery service (normally inside or near the DFA office itself-this can be done on the day of application itself.)

8. Contact the DFA office

Should the applicant not recieve their passport in the set amount of time, remember that it is your right to be able to contact and ask for clarity from the DFA office.
