General

Barangay clearance is a document that certify an individual that he/she is a resident of that barangay. More importantly, it also proves the applicant that he/she doesnâ€™t have any bad records and in good moral character.

Barangay clearance is one of the requirements needed when you are processing a legal documents or financial documents. Sometimes, it is being requested when you are applying for a job, ID application, supporting documents or any other legal purpose it that requires. This certificate is stamped and sealed with the Barangay Official Seal and signed by the Barangay. captain.

Procedures

1. Prepare all required documents

Applicants must have their NSO birth certificate, a filled-up application form (can be printed online), at least three (3) valid IDs, and other supporting documents that identify the applicant. Make sure to have at least two (2) photocopies of each documents.

2. Make an appointment

To set an appointment with the DFA, you can either go to the nearest DFA office or visit the DFA Appointment Website. Answer the application form, and reference number will be given to you after submission. Whether done online or in person, have your accomplished application form printed.

3. Appear on your scheduled appointment

Go to the DFA Consular Office on your scheduled date and time. If applicable, visit the most convenient DFA satellites and Philippine Embassy Consulate as per your appointment. Do not forget to present your application form and wait for your turn.

4. Go through the process

Once your queue numbers is called, you will be directed by a DFA official through a short process of finalizing their application.

5. Pay for your application

Applicant will be asked to pay a processing fee at the cashier. A fee of Php 950.00 for regular processing and will take about 25 working days exclusive of holidays. A rush processing is also available at Php 1,250.00 for only 15 working days exclusive of holidays. If the application is for a lost and valid passport, applicants will pay an additional Php 200.00 (please note that the DFA advises an allowance of 6 months prior to travel in getting a passport.)

6. Submit for recording

Proceed to encoding section and have your photo and other information taken by the staff.

7. Wait for your passport

The processing of your passport can take up to thirty (30) days. If available, you can proceed to the delivery counter and provide your delivery information, so you can have the passport delivered to you. You may also opt to claim your passport in person.

Should the applicant not recieve their passport.

Should the applicant not recieve their passport in the set amount of time, remember that it is your right to be able to contact and ask for clarity from the DFA office.

Additional Requirements for Minors

A. IF MINOR IS TRAVELLING WITH EITHER PARENT

  • Personal appearance of mother is required if minor is an illegitimate child.
  • Affidavit of support and consent (ASC) executed by either parent indicating the name of travelling companion and relationship to the minor. If minor will be travelling alone, ASC must be executed by either parent, stating that his/her child will be travelling alone. If minor is illegitimate, mother should execute ASC.
  • Original and photocopy of DSWD Clearance. There is no need to secure a DSWD clearance if the minor travelling aborad has parents who are in the foreign service or living abroad or are immigrants, provided he/she is holding a valid pass such as a dependent's visa/pass/identification card or permanent resident visa/pass/identification card which serves as proof that he/she is living with parents abroad
B. IF BOTH PARENTS ARE ABROAD

  • Affidavit of support and consent (ASC) executed by either parent indicating the name of travelling companion and relationship to the minor. If minor will be travelling alone, ASC must be executed by either parent, stating that his/her child will be travelling alone. If minor is illegitimate, mother should execute ASC.
  • Special power of attorney (SPA) with an attached photocopy of either parent's valid passport (authenticated by the nearest Philippine Embassy or Consulate General) authorizing a representative in assisting the child to apply for a passport. If minor is illegitimate, mother should execute the SPA.
  • Original and photocopy of DSWD clearance. There is no need to secure a DSWD clearance if the minor travelling abroad has parents who are in the foreign service of living abroad or are immigrants, provided he/she is holding a valid pass such as a dependent's visa/pass/identification card or permanent resident visa/pass/identification card which serves as proof that he/she is living with parents abroad.
  • Proper ID of duly authorized representative (Please refer to the list of acceptable IDs)
C. IF MINOR IS LEGITIMATED BY SUBSEQUENT MARRIAGE OF PARENTS

  • Authenticated Birth Certificate from NSO with annotation regarding new status as legitimated and the full name of the child.
D. IF MINOR IS ILLEGITIMATE BUT ACKNOWLEDGE BY FATHER

  • Birth certification from NSO reflecting surname of father with Affidavit of Acknowledgement and consent to use surname of father.
E. ORPHANED MINOR APPLICANT

  • Authenticated Death Certificate of parents from NSO
  • Court order of awarding guardianship of the orphaned minor applicant or substitute parental authority under Article 214 & 216 of the family code.
  • DSWD Clearance.
F. ABANDONED MINOR APPLICANT

  • Court order of awarding guardianship of the orphaned minor applicant or substitute parental authority.
  • DSWD Clearance
G. FOUNDLING

  • Certificate of foundling authenticated by NSO
  • DSWD Clearance
  • Passport of the person who found the applicant
  • Letter of authority or endorsement from DSWD for the issuance of passport
H. LEGALLY ADOPTED

  • Original and certified true copy (CTC) of NSO Birth Certificate before adoption
  • Original and certified true copy (CTC) of the NSO amended birth certificate after adoption
  • Certified true copy (CTC) of the court decision or oder on adoption and certificate of finality.
  • DSWD clearance for minor applicant, if travelling with the person
I. FOR ADOPTION BY FOREIGN PARENTS

  • Certified true copy of the court decree of abandonment of child
  • NSO Death certificate of the child's parents of the or the deed of voluntary commitment executed after the birth of the child
  • Endorsement of the child to the intercountry adoption board by the DSWD
  • Authenticated birth or foundling certificate
J. MINOR APPLICANT WHOSE PARENTS ARE ANULLED / DIVORCED

  • Court order awarding guardianship of the minor applicant or substitute parental authority
  • DSWD Travel Clearance
  • NSO Marriage Certificate with annoation on nullity or annulment decree

  • NSO Birth Certificate of minor applicant and mother
  • Affidavit of support and consent executed by the maternal grandparent/s indicating the name of travelling companion
  • Proof of identity of mother and maternal grandparent/s (Please refer to list of acceptable IDs)
