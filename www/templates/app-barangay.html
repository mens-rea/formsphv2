General

Barangay clearance is a document that certify an individual that he/she is a resident of that barangay. More importantly, it also proves the applicant that he/she doesn’t have any bad records and in good moral character.

Barangay clearance is one of the requirements needed when you are processing a legal documents or financial documents. Sometimes, it is being requested when you are applying for a job, ID application, supporting documents or any other legal purpose it that requires. This certificate is stamped and sealed with the Barangay Official Seal and signed by the Barangay. captain.

Procedures

1. Go to your local barangay hall

Find the barangay secretary and inform them that you need a barangay clearance. Be prepared to answer a few short questions about your residency and purpose for getting a barangay clearance.

2. Submit your accomplished form

Fill out and complete an application form with accurate details. Some barangays will only ask for your signature while others require a thumb print and will even take your picture.

3. Pay the necessary fees

After completing the application form and supplying your details, submit all the requirements and pay the processing fees. It is advised that you bring between P50-P100. Once paid, just wait for 20 to 30 minutes and you will be given your barangay clearance.

Requirements

Recent Cedula (Community Tax Certificate) Accomplished Application Form



Additional Requirement

Photocopy of any latest utility billing receipt under your name / relative’s name.
* might require for new residents or new applicants with no barangay records.
