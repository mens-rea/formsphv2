Procedures

1. Register your business

Register your business with the DTI and obtain a Business Name Registration Certificate.




2. Secure clearance

for barangay/municipal/regional clearance
Present your Business Name Registration Certificate, two (2) valid IDs and proof of address of business location to the concerned local government where you plan to setup the business.

3. Get a business permit

Visit the mayor's office and present your Business Name Registration Certificate, two (2) valid IDs, a map or sketch of the location of the business and your community tax certificate (in some cases).

4. Visit the BIR

Go to the Bureau of Internal Revenue (BIR) district office where your business will be located and present the following: Business Name Registration Certificate, Mayor's Permit, Barangay Clearance, Proof of your business address (e.g. lease/rental agreement, land title), Book of Accounts, and receipts and invoices from BIR accredited print shops, to be stamped by the BIR.

5. Apply for TIN and SSS

As a requirement, you are required to secure a Tax Identification Number and apply as a self-employed member of the Social Security System.

6. Pay for your application

After the application has been turned over to the cashier, you are required to pay for the fees.

7. Secure other permits

If necessary, you must secure other permits for your business. Present your paid application to the Bureau of Fire Protection (BFP), Health and Sanitary Office, and City Environment and Natural Resources Office (CENRO) to receive your fire permit, sanitary permit, and environment permit respectively.

8. Claim your business permit

Once the process is done, you will receive your license to operate. Take note that in some cities, permits may be issued differently.
